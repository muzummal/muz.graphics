Recipe Box Application Design Process

A recipe management app to help users organize, and share their recipes.

Overview

Summary

Recipe Box is an app to help users organize recipes. Additionally, users can use the app to create macro goals, get recipe ideas, and share recipes.

Users do not have an adequate recipe management application. Recipe Box intends to improve the recipe management app to allow users to add and share recipes with ease.

TOC

  • Understand
  • Product Design
  • User Tests
  • Reiterate

Role

UX/UI Designer, Graphic Designer, Brand Manager

Tools & Technology

Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, CMS, HTML, CSS

Target

Home cooks, meal preppers, people with health goals

Understand

Research

I began my research by interviewing professionals and home cooks with the following questions:

  • Do you consider yourself a food enthusiast?
  • Do you use recipes, why or why not?
  • How do you save recipes that you like?

Pain Points

  • Increasing recipe yield can be difficult to calculate with lots of ingredients
  • Keeping track of nutrition information can be a hassle
  • Finding substitutes and where you can buy those substitutes

Competitive Audit

I looked at similar apps that offer recipe management and they focus heavily on other goals. I noticed their primary focus is usually weight loss and they do not offer extensive recipe management features. I want to create the Recipe Box app to help users save recipes in a digital format similar to a traditional recipe box. This way adding recipes passed down through generations would be easier to manage in comparison to other apps.

Personas

I discussed this topic with close friends and colleagues who were willing to help me with testing and research. I used the information I gathered to create personas.

Leslie

Leslie loves her job and is a very organized person. Her organization allows her to get a lot done. She finds that recipes are a great way to help maintain a healthy lifestyle, but has a hard time using apps with recipe management features because they do not provide ingredient substitutes for diets or allergens.

Goals

  • Wants to keep recipes in one location
  • Wants to easily understand nutritional information

Frustrations

  • Not having recipes easily accessible
  • Not being able to find substitutes

Demitri

Demitri is a smart guy who learns fast. His goal in life is to be a concept artist, but falls on being a graphic designer in order to make money. Another goal that he is also focused on is staying in shape and building muscle mass. He understands working out, but nutrition can be more difficult.

Goals

  • Wants to keep track of protein intake
  • Working on bulking then cutting

Frustrations

  • Not being able to get enough protein in a day
  • Recipe suggestions not personal

Insight

It is important for users to understand the nutritional breakdown of recipes. Creating a place to store recipes is the primary goal, and a well-written nutritional page compliments the recipe management.

Product Design

Recipe Box is designed to add and organize recipes so they are easy to manage. A key feature is a functionality that will allow the user to adjust the yield results which will automatically update serving size and nutritional information. The main page would be a personal feed that would inspire the user to customize shared recipes. The green and orange colors were chosen to reflect the brand’s health conscious vision. The colors of the design would give fresh food vibes with vibrant greens and oranges that help illustrate the focus on health and wellness.

Testing

Usability Study

While supervising the user tests, I learned about some user expectations that I didn’t anticipate. Conducting user tests helped me refine new UI elements I wanted to try.

Questions and Tasks

  • What would you click to create a new recipe?

    • Would that be the process you normally take?
  • Are the icons easily readable?

  • How would you see the nutritional information of a recipe you created?

    • Is there something you would like to see that would make it easier for organizing or creating recipes?

Results

I made the mistake of trying to create too many page variations. It was not practical to try and create an example for each saved recipe. During the testing, I realized I couldn’t simultaneously test all the features and needed to focus on testing one feature at a time before moving onto the next. My focus needed to be on creating a seamless system to add recipes, simplify data organization, and allow access to nutritional information.

In moving onto the hi-fi prototype, I corrected my focus and incorporated the seamless system design concepts mentioned above. I also added color to this prototype to help convey the vibe of a healthy environment. This environment encourages users to add and share healthful recipes and use the goals section to establish and track overall healthy lifestyle choices. Finally, I added more animations to deliver a live app experience.

Reiterate

Mock Ups

Testing and prototyping helped me find two issues I did not want to carry over to a hi-fi prototype. Users noted they expected to see an edit button because managing a recipe would be pointless if you could not edit the recipe itself.

Additionally, users wanted command action feedback to know their action is successful. Going forward, I would use animations in the lo-fi prototype to show command action feedback.

Accessibility

Smooth and seamless interface navigation is extremely important for the user experience, Therefore I decided to focus on the sandwich menu functionality. Even the best designed icons could result in accessibility issues. For example, some users might struggle due to regional differences, age, visual impairment, or digital illiteracy.

To improve accessibility for icons I would work with web developers to make sure the icons have appropriate alternative texts for screen readers.

Conclusion

Take Away

Going through the user testing on the mock ups reinforced the importance of user feedback. For a lo-fi prototype, it isn’t necessary to create all the pages, but the key features needed to get a better idea of what the application offers.

The process of testing, designing and refining allowed me to learn how I can make future tests easier for the user, prioritize accessibility, and be patient and intentional about the next step.

Final Design

Next Steps

My next steps would be to create and test the design for the key features such as an overview page of the recipes, nutrition page, and recipe sharing function. I would also like to see a dark mode and how this design would translate into a web application.
