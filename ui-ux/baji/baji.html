Baji Web Design, Research, and
Brand Development

A big sister program that provides community children with the guidance, support, care, and love that comes from a big sister.

Overview

Summary

Baji was created as a program curriculum template. Baji’s core focus is to provide “big brother/sister” guidance for young Muslims.

Baji needs a website to help promote the brand and its offerings to Muslim communities that need help in building outreach programs and to showcase the curriculum Baji has to offer.

Understand

Research

I started my search by conducting community member interviews. Some of the questions I included:

  • What are some opportunities and services missing in your communities?
  • What is the most important issue that affects the youth community?

Pain Points

  • Find no engagement from masjids and Islamic centers
  • Are limited to just one activity or lesson for kids
  • Wait for holidays to bring the community together

Competitive Audit

When I conducted the research, I looked at the business models and functions of the community-run programs. Most of the models were self-run with guidance on how to start your own branch. I thought this was the best model to use because it would allow communities to customize the model based on their needs.

Personas

After interviewing members in Muslim communities, I constructed interviewee personas.

Samaya

Samaya is a recent college graduate who just came back from volunteer work with the United Nations. She wants to do more for her community, but she is having a hard time finding resources, networking, and bringing a community together.

Goals

  • Create a community based on Islamic values
  • Have resources and organizational structures to organize activities

Frustrations

  • Not knowing where to begin in starting a community
  • Having to create activities from scratch

Ebrahim

Ebrahim wants to move to a new city in the US. He is not worried about finding a job because he works remotely. What he really wants is to find a city with a good Muslim community that reflects his values. Doing searches on popular search engines returned results with mosques and populations count, but he did not learn valuable information about the communities.

Goals

  • Find a community that aligns with his values
  • Help the community and improve his environment

Frustrations

  • Being unable to know which communities provide programs
  • Joining a community in a new city

Product Design

I created a layout with a splash image. Each page included a persona to represent diverse users. I had to consider each page needed to display different content and information. Lastly, I also considered ways to have the layout best convey the information in an easy-to-read manner.

User Tests

Usability Study

I conducted an unsupervised study which included a list of tasks and questions. In doing so, I learned drawbacks and positives, which helped me plan the project direction.

Questions and Tasks

  • What would you click to get to the Programs page?
  • Is the information easily readable?
  • What are your thoughts on accessing the Programs page?

Results

From the lo-fi prototype testing I learned what would be impactful for this and future projects.

One thing I learned is that it would be more helpful for users to have a partial product description instead of mock up text such as Lorem Ipsum.

Another issue that stood out was the pages did not seem like they were from the same website. Even though this was just a wireframe, I think the pages were too inconsistent.

Reiterate

Mock Ups

I implemented my results into my design. I was presented with a new learning experience each time I moved from a wireframe to a mockup. There were only a few areas where I had to make changes to improve the design and readability.

Accessibility

In the design process I was able to find an error that caused an accessibility problem. Particularly the navigation menu being difficult to read. My original plan when creating the wireframe was to make the text white so that it would contrast the image, but finding the perfect image for each page proved to be more difficult. This would also be a strain on the designer when refreshing images in the future because they would have to spend more time and resources on images.

To solve this problem, I increased the legibility of the navigation links by creating a contrasting background. This also helped the appearance of the logo, account icon, and options link. As is generally the case with accessibility features, designing a better experience for those who need specialized functionalities improves the experience for everyone.

Conclusion

Take Away

Taking an old logo concept I made and turning it into an actual product was a fun project, and a good learning experience. Using information from my notes on basic user needs and wants allowed me to find better accessibility options, create a consistent experience, and overall, design a product that is functional and true to the brand.

The process of testing, designing, and refining allowed me to learn ways to break up large project tasks into smaller organized steps that improves the speed and quality. I am confident I would be accessibility focused on future projects.

Final Design

Next Steps

The next steps for this project would be to make this project mobile friendly. Studies show more people start their search on mobile devices. I would have to consider content priority and information priority because designing for mobile experience poses the challenge of limited space. For example, I would highlight programs offered by Baji over Baji branches. In hindsight, I should have done a mobile-first design, and used an additive process to improve the mobile experience.
